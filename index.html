Symphony Rendering: MIDI & Composer-Conditioned Auto Orchestration with Flow-Matching Transformers

Code

Abstract

We introduce Symphony Rendering, the task of transforming a melody input (MIDI or piano solo) into high-fidelity symphonic audio under explicit composer conditioning. Unlike text-to-music generation, the input and output are tightly time-aligned: pitch and rhythm must be preserved while instrumentation, articulation, and timbre are rendered by the model. We present a flow-matching transformer that conditions on frame-level musical controls and a composer embedding, enabling a single melody to be realized in styles such as “Mahler,” “Beethoven,” or “Tchaikovsky” without retraining. The system unifies orchestration and timbral rendering and is designed to scale with additional composers and control signals. Audio demonstrations and reproducible code are provided on this page.
